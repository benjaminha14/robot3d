Team 254




Dropshot 2016

Dropshot offers a robust drivetrain that is capable crossing the defenses and an accurate, lightning fast shooter. Dropshot will compete at the Central Valley Regional, the Silicon Valley Regional, and at the FIRST Championship in St. Louis, Missouri.

Deadlift 2015

Team 254 competed with its robot, Deadlift, at the Central Valley Regional, the Silicon Valley Regional and at the FIRST Championship. After a highly successful season and regional showings, Team 254 went on to reach the Carson quarterfinals at the World Championship.

Barrage 2014

The 2014 season was possibly Team 254’s most successful yet. After a winning streak of three regional events, the team went on to win the World Championship, going undefeated through the qualification matches. Along the way, the team won a number of awards and later won Team 254’s offseason tournament, Chezy Champs. One achievement from 2014 that we are particularly proud of was the Gracious Professionalism award which is given to, “[Celebrate] outstanding demonstration of FIRST Core Values”.

Overkill 2013

In 2013, Team 254 built a robot that excelled at nearly all aspects of Ultimate Ascent, a technically challenging game. Through the hard work the team put in during the build season, Team 254 was the captain of the winning alliance at the San Diego and Silicon Valley Regionals.

Skyfire 2012

Eager to build on last years accomplishments, the team began work with the 2012 FRC challenge Rebound Rumble with their robot, Skyfire. As finalists at the Central Valley Regional and champions at the Silicon Valley Regional, the team showcased its skill and engineering, finishing the season as finalists in the Curie Division at the FRC World Championship.

Slipstream 2011

The FRC game Logomotion marks a very successful season for Team 254 thanks to the engineering and design that went into their robot Slipstream. With victories at the Silicon Valley Regional and the San Diego Regional, the team went on to win the First Robotics Competition Championship, along with the championship Industrial Design Award.

Onslaught 2010

Team 254 designed Onslaught for the FRC 2010 season game, Breakaway. Onslaught went on to win two regional events as well as the Archimedes division at the FIRST World Championship.
